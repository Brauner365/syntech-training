DOES AEI NEED A LICENCE?

Yes — immediately relevant

To hire any candidate without UK right to work, a sponsor licence is required. Syntech has candidates from Poland, Germany, Brazil, and Ghana in the current AEI pipeline. One urgent live case: James Nowak's Skilled Worker visa expires in 40 days.EXAMPLE DATA

1

Eligibility check and key personnel

Confirm AEI is a genuine UK-trading entity. Appoint the Authorising Officer (AO) — a senior employee or director legally responsible for the licence — and at least one Level 1 User to operate the SMS.

2

HR systems and compliance audit

Before applying, AEI must have documented, operational processes for right-to-work checks, personnel record-keeping per Appendix D, and a system for reporting changes within 10 working days.

Inadequate HR systems are the most common reason for post-grant licence revocation.
3

Gather supporting documents — Appendix A

Certificate of Incorporation; employer's liability insurance (minimum £5m cover); UK business bank statements (last 3 months); VAT registration certificate; latest filed accounts; organisation chart.

4

Submit online via UKVI portal

Fee paid at submission — non-refundable on refusal. Specify routes required and estimated year-one CoS allocation. The Authorising Officer must personally make the application.

5

Processing and outcome

Standard: ~8 weeks. Priority (+£750): ~10 working days. On approval, AEI receives an A-rated licence valid 4 years with automatic renewal.

6

Assign Certificates of Sponsorship per hire

For each sponsored worker, AEI assigns a CoS via the SMS. The worker uses the CoS reference to apply for their visa. AEI pays the CoS fee (£525) and Immigration Skills Charge — both tracked in the Syntech portal.
